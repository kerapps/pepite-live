Three things you must know today, then the good stuff underneath. The labs are fighting on price now, not just power. OpenAI loosened the limits on Codex. Anthropic shipped a model that costs double for 5.7 percent more. Mistral is raising at a €20 billion valuation. The pattern under all of it is simple. The race is moving from what models can do to what they cost. Below the news, the usual gems. Tools that make your agent behave.

OpenAI kicks off the AI price wars with flexible rate-limit resets for its Codex coding agent↗

OpenAI changed how Codex rate limits work. You can now bank your resets and trigger them by hand. Hit your cap mid-session? Cash in a saved reset instead of waiting it out. Free users get one. Plus and Pro users earn more by inviting friends. The Decoder frames it as the start of a price war. It's really a usage tweak, not a price cut. But if Codex limits keep breaking your flow, this helps. And it shows where the pressure is: coding agents, on cost.

Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 costs twice as much for 5.7 percent more performance↗

Claude Fable 5 is the new top model on the Artificial Analysis index. It sets records in five of ten benchmarks. Here's the catch. It beats Opus 4.8 by just 5.7 percent. And it costs twice as much per token. Safety routing pushes the bill higher still. The lesson is old but worth repeating. The flagship is rarely the best value. Test the cheaper model on your own tasks before you pay double for a few points you may never feel.

Post · TechCrunch AI

Mistral is rumored to be raising €3B at €20B valuation↗

Mistral is reportedly raising €3 billion. That would value it near €20 billion, almost double its last round. Why it matters to builders: Mistral is Europe's main open-weight lab. A war chest this size keeps its open models funded and shipping. Good news for anyone who wants an alternative to the US labs. The caveat: it's still a rumor, and a valuation is not a better model. Nothing about your API calls changes today. But the open-weight lane just got more secure.

●Fresh · 2h old

✎ Builder's Tip of the Day

Don't auto-upgrade to the newest flagship model. Today's Fable 5 news is the reminder. It costs double for 5.7 percent more on benchmarks. And benchmarks are not your workload. Keep a small set of your real tasks with known-good answers. Run the new model on it. Switch only if the gain shows up on your tasks, not someone else's leaderboard.

Repo · GitHub

DietrichGebert/ponytail↗

You know the senior dev who deletes your fifty lines and writes one? ponytail tries to be that dev for your agent. It runs inside Claude Code or Cursor. When the agent wants to over-build, it pushes back. You asked for a date picker, not a debate about timezones. Think YAGNI as a prompt. It's four days old with 773 stars, so some of the buzz is just novelty. But the problem is real. Agents love to over-build, and few tools fight it. Read the README. You'll know in thirty seconds if it's for you.

Repo · GitHub

amElnagdy/guard-skills↗

This is the other half of ponytail, and maybe the better half. guard-skills reviews your agent's output before you do. It looks for the usual ways AI code goes wrong. The confident but broken test. The doc that describes code that isn't there. The flow is simple. Let the agent work. Then run the right guard on the diff before you commit. It's prompt-based, so it won't catch everything a type-checker would. But as a cheap second opinion in Claude Code, Codex, or Cursor, it earns its place fast.

Paper · arXiv

Reward Modeling for Multi-Agent Orchestration↗

Running many agents? You've hit the eval wall. How do you tell a good run from a lucky one without grading each by hand? OrchRM has an idea. Use the artifacts a multi-agent run already produces. Turn them into win/lose pairs. Train a reward model on those. No human labels. No costly re-runs. It's a preprint and a work in progress, so treat the numbers as a claim, not proof. But it ships code. And the core idea is right: score the orchestration, not the sub-tasks.
